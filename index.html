Streams22: A workshop for assembling a community atlas of stellar streams
October 31 &ndash November 4, 2022

Community Atlas of Tidal Streams

Motivation Scope Logistics Apply

Why? Motivation

Recent years have seen an enormous growth in the discovery of tidal streams in the Milky Way, reaching nearly 100 known streams today. This unprecedented data set provides an avenue to study some of the first stars in the Universe and presents some of the most stringent tests of the dark-matter paradigm. To deliver on these goals, a homogeneous catalog of all known streams is needed – as identified by the community during the Streams21 online workshop.

What? Workshop Scope

The aim of the Streams22 workshop is to produce the first atlas of tidal streams in the Milky Way, including detailed membership probabilities of individual stars for each stream. With this goal in mind, this meeting will be an interactive workshop and working meeting, not a traditional conference. At the end of the workshop we will release the first version of the atlas on the arXiv, and build on this work in the following weeks and months before submitting to a peer-reviewed journal. The publication will not only report on the methods and procedures for generating the atlas, but also provide a template to follow for the reporting of future stream discoveries, thus creating a lasting legacy. In addition, we will produce a web interface for the atlas on Flathub to provide easy access to the Milky Way streams to newcomers to the field.

How? Workshop format

The aim of the meeting is to bring together the world-leading experts in the discovery, characterisation, and modeling of tidal streams for a week-long workshop to produce a community-led atlas of tidal streams. The meeting will be held in-person at the Carnegie Theoretical Astrophysics Center at the Carnegie Observatories. This offers a collaborative, interactive workspace including the new VizLab ideal for visualizing the newly-created streams atlas. As the aims of the workshop are very focussed, the meeting will be limited to around 20 in-person participants.

Participants will span the required range of expertise, including in the analysis of stellar populations, astrometric data, spectroscopy, specific distance tracers, and dynamical modeling. Prior to the workshop, we expect to identify specific goals for each of these core components, seek team leaders from a pool of already identified experts, and let participants self-organize into 4–5 person teams. Most of the in-person time at the meeting will be dedicated to collaboratively working within teams towards their individual goals. At the close of each day we will come together and each team will report on the achievements of the day as well as flag any issues encountered.

While the meeting will be conducted primarily in person, we want to include contributions to the atlas from everyone interested. There will also be an opportunity for online participation, coordinated individually by working groups.

We expect all participants to abide by the Carnegie Code of Conduct.

There is funding available for travel and lodging support and there are no registration fees associated with the workshop.

When? Schedule

The workshop will take place from Monday October 31 to Friday November 4 2022. Each day will run from 9am until 5pm PDT (UTC+9). We will announce a detailed schedule nearer the time.

Get involved Apply

We are currently seeking applications for the workshop. Applicants should fill out this Google Form. The deadline for application is July 31 2022.

Participants will be selected to ensure the necessary range of expertise is covered such that the goals of the workshop can be achieved. We envisage requiring expertise in the analysis of stellar populations, astrometric data, spectroscopy, specific distance tracers (e.g. RR Lyrae, BHB, RGB), data access and manipulation, and dynamical modeling. We also hope to provide remote channels for participating but this is primarily an in-person meeting.

What next? Legacy

We hope this workshop will lead to new ideas and research directions. During this workshop we plan to keep a written record of the discussions through a collaborative Google Doc and develop the Atlas as a community-driven product.

In the longer term we would like to continue this topical meeting series, so stay tuned about future announcements, or get in touch if you would like to propose ideas for future meetings or to help coordinate these meetings.
